Third- The Rev. Mr. Elwood, on Sun-
day night, preached a sermon on
" Should the Murderer Be Lynched?"
He advocated lynching- only in case of
a failure of justice, it is true- but in
the inflamed condition of popular sentiment
one can imagine the influence of
any justification of mob violence coming
from the pulpit of a Christian
church. In his defense, telegraphed to
the Herald this morning, Mr. Elwood
throws all the blame on the judges.
His" sermon" of Sunday night was in
line with the policy of a steadily increasing
class of preachers who, in
their morbid efforts to excite" sensation
" and achieve publicity, are creating
a" yellow pulpit" that is worse than
the" yellow press.
