While the crime of Mitchell, the Ur-
bana fiend who was lynched by a mob
in that city, was a fearful one, the
action of the mob was such as to make
a bad matter much worse and to bring
shame and disgrace upon the fair name
of the city. Mitchell was in the keep-
ing of the officers of the law, and was
sentenced to the full limit of the law
prescribed for such crimes as his.
Whether the punishment would fit the
crime or not, was not for a mob to
decide- it was the law, and should have
been permitted to take its course- and
the members of that mob are guilty of
murder. It is gratifying to know that
