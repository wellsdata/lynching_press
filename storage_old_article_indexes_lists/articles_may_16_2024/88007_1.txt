He Feared to Be Lynched.
It was developed in testimony at the in-.
quest at Cincinnati, Ohio, on the death of
Alfred Quick, who killed himself, that the
motive was to escape mob vengeance.
Quick had been discovered in the attempt
to assault a girl ten years old, and with the
Urbana incident fresh in his mind, took his
own rather than risk death at the
hands life of a mob.
