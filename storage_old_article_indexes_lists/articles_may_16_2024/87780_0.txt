THREE NEGROES LYNCHED
IN ALABAMA
TUSCALLOOSA, Ala., Aug. 14.
( P) One of the three negroes
spirited away by the lynching
party is still missing, after the
bodies of his two companions were
found riddled with bullets. Dan
Pippin, Jr. aged 18. Elmore Clark,
aged 28, and A. T. Harden, aged
16. were indicted for killing Miss
Vaudine Maddox, aged 21. The
white police said that as a result
of rumors of an attack on the jail
here, they started to move the
prisoners to Birmingham for safe
keeping. The mob stopped the
officers enroute and took the pri.
soners from them Pippin
and Harden's away bodies were found.
