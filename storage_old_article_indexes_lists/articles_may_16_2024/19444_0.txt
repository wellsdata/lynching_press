WILL SUE NEW MADRID COUNTY.
Friends of Negro Who Was
Lynched Are Seeking Damages.
REPUBLIC SPECIAL
Chicago, Ill., Feb. 27.- Negro and white
citizens of Chicago will seek redress on be
half of the family of Lewis F. Wright, the
negro minstrel who was lynched on the
night of February 15, at New Madrid, Mo.
An effort will be made to make the county
of New Madrid, of which the town of New
Madrid is the county seat, pay for the
negro's death.
A large citizens' committee, composed of
whites and negroes who live in the neighborhood
of West Lake street, Hermitage
avenue and vicinity, the locality in which the
Wright boy was born and reared, has been
organized and already raised a large sum
money with which to employ counsel in
the of matter.
