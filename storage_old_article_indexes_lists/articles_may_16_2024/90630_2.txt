( From Chicago Journal, Sept. 14.)
The Journal many times has called attention
to the wickedness of lynching, to the strong
probability that the lynchers will get the
wrong man, to the lawlessness, disorder and
contempt for orderly processes which the custom
engenders.
The Associated Press dispatch quoted below
shows another side to lynching, namely, its
utter senselessness:
