St. Paul, Minn., was the scene of a
cold blooded murder on the 19th. While T.
J. Law, owner of the barges on the river was
talking with a woman in her door yard, Otto
Cavensky a Pole, shot him through the back,
killing him instantly. Last April Lavensky's
son, while working on Law's barge, fell overboard
and was drowned. Lavensky swore
vengeance on Law, and procuring a gun,
loaded it with ball and buckshot, and laid in
wait for his victim. Lavensky confessed the
crime when and manifested no regret
. The murderer arrested is liable to be lynched.
