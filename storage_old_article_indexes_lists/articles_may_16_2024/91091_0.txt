GALVESTON, July 17.- On the evening of the
15th, a riot commenced at Millican, a station on
the Central railroad. A mob of twenty- five ne-
groes, led by a white school teacher and a negro
preacher named Brooks, attempted to hang
a man named William Holeday. The white citizens
prevented the execution, and, headed by
the sheriff and the agent of the freedmen's
bureau, attempted to suppress the mob; the at-
resulted in the death of ten or twelve of
tempt the negroes.
