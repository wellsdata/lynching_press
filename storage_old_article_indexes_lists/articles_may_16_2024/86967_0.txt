Accuse Innocent Men.
GAINESVILLE, Tex., Jan. 30.- Eight
prominent farmers of Healdsbury, I. T.,
were arrested in Indian Territory, and
brought here to- day charged with being
members of the mob which lynched an
old man in Indian Territory last Novem
ber. The United States commissioner directed
them taken to Paris, Tex., for
trial by the United States district court.
which has jurisdiction over Indian Territory
. The principal witnesses against the
prisoners are said to be notorious horse
thieves who themselves are responsible
for the lynching.
It is alleged they accused
the farmers now under arrest of
crime in order to divert suspicion from
themselves. Much indignation exists in
the territory, and talk of lynching
accusers is freely indulged in. the
