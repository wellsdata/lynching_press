TEXAS MURDERER LYNCHED
Mob Completes Work Which Jury by
Acquittal Begins.
A Port Arthur, Tex., special says
that James Sweeney. white. was
lynched at that place for murdering
Charles Crumbach, a laborer in a cotton
mill over which Sweeney presided
as foreman.
The murder occurred February 1,
Sweeney was indicted and at his trial
at Beaumont was acquitted. When he
returned to Port Arthur a mob met
him at the depot, took him up town
and hanged him to a telephone pole.
