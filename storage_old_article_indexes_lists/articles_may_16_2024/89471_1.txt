Great Crowd at the Scene
As soon as it became known that
Frank had been lynched, hundreds of
automobiles containing the morbidly
curious hurried to the scene. By 11
o'clock it was estimated that 5000
people were about the tree. The body
wasn't cut down until a number of
speeches had been made. One man
urged the mutilation of the body.
Judge Morris urged that order be observed
the crowd then voted against
mutilation. The body was placed
in
a wagon to be taken to Marietta for
an inquest, but Judge Morris, fearing
threats of mutilation, transferred it
to his automobile and sped to Atlanta
00 automobiles pursuing.,
