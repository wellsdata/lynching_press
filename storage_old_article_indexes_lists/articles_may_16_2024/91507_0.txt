RED BANK, N. J., July 15.- Prompt
action on the part of the chief of police
saved Thomas Thomas, a negro,
from being lynched by citizens of that
place roused to fury by the negro's brutal
attack upon Miss Grace Davis,
daughter of the mayor of Red Bank,
and known throughout the state for her
beauty. Thomas, who is a former convict
, attacked Miss Davis while he was
robbing her father's home. The burglar
struck the girl repeatedly in an effort
to beat her into insensibility. Citizens
learned of this soon after his arrest
and made threats of lynching him
or burning him at the stake. Chief of
Police Wilson outwitted them and took
negro quickly out of town to the
jail the at Freehold.
