The indignation and the excitement were
such that mob law and lynching were
about to be resorted to. The entire community
felt that in the endeavor to reach
the guilty parties, innocent Italians might
be sacrificed. To allay excitement, and to
assist the constituted authorities, the
mayor of the city appointed a committee
of fifty representative citizens to take
charge of the investigation and to aid in
the trial and conviction of the assassins.
This measure arrested violence. The committee
