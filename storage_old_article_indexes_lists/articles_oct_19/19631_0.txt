MOB LYNCHED CLERGYMAN.
German Paper Cites Incident as One
of Many Atrocities.
-
Copenhagen, Sept. 14.- Evidence of
atrocities perpetrated upon innocent
civilians by Germans comes from
Ferlin. The Socialist organ Vorwaert
draws attention to the shocking case
of a Belgian clergyman who was
lynched at Liebenstein, in the Duchy
of Saxemeiningen. He was taken
prisoner and was marched to a military
depot. A crowd suddenly broke
through the cordon of soldiers and
sarrounded him. They accused him
of being an inciter of supposed Bel
gian outrages on Germans. He was
carried away and lynched. Another
prisoner was attacked by a mob.
complains that these out
Vorwaerts rages are increasing.
