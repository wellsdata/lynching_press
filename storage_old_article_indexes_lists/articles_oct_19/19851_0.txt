SUPREME COURT NOTICES
LYNCHING OF JOHNSON
Attorney General Moody, in the Supreme
Court of the United States, this
afternoon called the attention of the
count to the recent lynching of Ed Johnson
at Chattanooga, and
the alleged
contempt of court of the sheriff and
his assistants and the members of the
mob that participated in that crime.
Chief Justice Fuller took cognizance
of the complaint and set it for
on the second Monday in the next hearing term.
