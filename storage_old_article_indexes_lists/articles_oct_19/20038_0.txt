L'OPINIONE publishes a dispatch which it
received from Palermo, and which contains
the information that an American journalist
named Engelmann, who has arrived in
that city, has been delegated by the govern
ment of the United States to render assistance
to the families of the Italian subjects
who were lynched in New Orleans. The
correspondent of L'Opinione asserts that
he has seen Engelmann, and it is stated
that Engelmann has paid a visit to the village
of Caccamo for the purpose of giving
help to the family of Lonasterio, one of
