A Charleston( S. C.) dispatch of the 11th
says that 16 negroes were arrested in Abbe
ville County, charged with the murder of
two white men, Allen and Baker, near
Lowndesville, and with having formed a conspiracy
to murder the white inhabitants of
the village. They were placed under guard
and started for Anderson, and it was re
ported that, while en route, they were captured
, and 13 of the negroes lynched.
later report, however, says that the negroes
were lodged safely in jail at Walhalla, with
the exception of one, who committed
eide. sui
