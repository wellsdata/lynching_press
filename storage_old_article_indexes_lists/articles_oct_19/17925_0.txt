Juror Has Good Excuse.
SPRINGFIELD, Mo., Aug. 8 .-- The
trial of the men eharged with the
lynching of several negroes here several
months ago was begun today.
Soon after the trial was begun, J. L.
Maxey, one of the regular panel of
jurors, received word that his home,
fifteen miles west of Springfield, had
been struck by lightning, his two children
killed and house destroyed.
He was excused from the further service.
