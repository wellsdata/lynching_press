MINNESOTA POLITICS- LYNCH LAW- CROPS
ST. PAUL'S, Minn., Aug. 24.- The State Convention
is progressing slowly. Some points of differ
ence have been harmoized, while others of importance
are unsettled. The Republicans favor the
a loption of one constitution, while the Democrats
urge the suspension of both.
The three half breed Indians, who killed the
German at Gull Lake last week, were taken from
the hands of the sheriff by the populace, and hung
to a tree.
The crops, notwithstanding the ravages of the
grasshoppers. are considered better than was
