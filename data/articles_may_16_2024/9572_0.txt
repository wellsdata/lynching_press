Still Under Federal Supervision.
GUTHRIE: Questioning the authority
of the federal court of the western
district of Oklahoma to try a case
wherein a crime was committed under
territorial government, and particularly
in the Osage nation, Attorneys Leahy
and Scott of Pawhuska,
somewhat of a sensation produced in Judge
Cotteral's court. These men represent
A. C. Green, Mike White and Frank
Williams, who are charged with
lynching a negro at Osage, July 16,
1907.
Representing the United States, the
assistant United States attorney, in
arguing the case contended that the
Jurisdiction of the federal government
should not be questioned, that the
crime was committed in the territory
under control of the federal government
and that the Osage nation is
under supervision of the federal
authorities still.
