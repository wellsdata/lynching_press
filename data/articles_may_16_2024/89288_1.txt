At La Crosse, Wis., Frank A. Burton
was shot dead on Fourth street by a river
man named Nathaniel Mitchell. The assassin
fired seven times, the first shot proving
fatal. He was soon placed in jail, about
which thousands of citizens gathered and
demanded him. The doors were forced and
the murderer lynched before midnight. Burton
was a leading business man, and President
of the and Logan Club. He
leaves a wife and Blaine three children.
