REPORTED FOR THE DAILY ARGUS.
NEW YORK, Jan. 30.- The N. Y. Herald's
Richmond special says: A dispatch from
Goldsboro, N. C., states that the greatest
excitement prevails at Kinston in couse-
quence of the lynching of five prisoners
there on Saturday night. A person near
the scene heard the discharging of the pistols
as the prisoners were shot on the bridge,
and heavy splashes of the bodies as they
were thrown into the river. Kinston is in a
state of siege by the negroes of the surrounding
country, who threaten to liberate
the remaining prisoners in jail and burn the
town. The whites are all armed with such
weapons as are at their command, guarding
their homes. are stationed in the
suburbs at night Pickets.
