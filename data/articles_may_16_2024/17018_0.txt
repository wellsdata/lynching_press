MURDER AND LYNCH LAW AT COUNCIL
BLUFF. A man named J. C. Samuels, from
Columbus, Ohio, was recently brutally murdered
and robbed at an encampment of Califor-
nia emigrants, near Council Bluff. A man
called Baltimore Muer was arrested by the
sheriff and taken to the court house on suspicion
of committing the deed. The incensed
emigrants, however, soon after his arrest, forei
bly took him from the custody of the sheriff,
gave him an immediate trial according to the
code of Judge Lynch, found him guilty, and at
5 o'clock the same afternoon hung him from
the limb of a tree. He protested his
to the last. innocence
