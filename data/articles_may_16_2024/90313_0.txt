A LIVELY BATTLE.
Negroes and Militlamen Enjoy a
Scrimmage.
WINSTON, N. C., Aug. 12.- It was announced
in the colored churches yesterday
that a mob would lynch Arthur
Tuttle, colored, on trial for the murder
of a policeman. One hundred and fifty
negroes marched to the jail armed with
shotguns. The sheriff ordered out the
Forsy the riflemen and the negroes fired
on them, wounding several. The riflemen
fired several volleys at the negroes,
who dispersed carrying off the wounded
. Fourteen, were arrested.
