Lynching and a Race War.
BIRMINGHAM, Ala., July 20.- A special
to the State Herald says: A race war is on
at Riverton, Ala., a town of 600 inhabitants,
and serious results are feared. A negro attempted
a criminal assault on Mrs. S. L.
Vaughan, an estimable white woman, Mon-
day afternoon, but was beaten off and the
neighborhood aroused. A searching party
is said to have caught the fiend and lynched
him, but this they did not admit. After
the lynching the whites became incensed
and threatened to exterminate the negroes.
Both sides are arming, and one white man
has been seriously injured. Mrs. Vaughan
i in a delicate condition and her death
imentarily expected. is
