LYNCHER TAKEN
TO STATE PRISON
THE MILITARY IS WITHDRAWN
AND ALL IS ONCE MORE QUIET
IN SALISBURY- ONE MORE TO
BE TRIED.
Charlotte, N. C., August 11.- George
Hall, the white man convicted yesterday
at Salisbury of conspiracy, in having led
the mob which lynched the three negro
murderers of the Lyerly family, and who
was sentenced to fifteen years, was removed
tonight to the state penitentiary
under a guard of state soldiers, George
Gentle, against whom there is a charge
of murder in the same connection, was
taken with Hall. The other prisoners have
been discharged. Gentle's case will be
taken up August 27.
The three military companies which
have been doing duty at the jail there
withdrawn tonight and all is quiet
were again.
