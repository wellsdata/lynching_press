PURSUING LIBERTY UNDER DIFFICULTIES.- Oa
the 11th inst., the conductor of a railroad train at
Millen, Georgia, discovered a negro under the body
of the car, banging by the frame work there, having
ridden in that position all the way from Macon
Georgia. He belonged to Mr. Dean, of Macon, and
bad, on a former occasion, taken passage under
the guards of the steamer Key Stone State, from
Savannah to Philadelphia, but was detected before
the boat left. He was handed over to his master.
Jas Costello, Dan Callaghan, John Shields and
Kennedy were sentenced to death in New
Patrick Orleans Saturday.
