LYNCHED. DASTARD A
GILMAN, W. T., Jan. 9.- At daylight
Monday morning Alfred Shafford, a Bohemian
miner, crawled under the residence
of George Bodayla, placed a keg of giant
powder there, and fired the fuse. The explosion
blew the house to atoms, killing
three persons and seriously wounded three
others. Shafford was arrested, but was
taken from officers and lynched.
