[ From the San Francisco Herald, June 11.].
We give, elsewhere, a report of the lynching of Jen-
kins, by the citizens, at 2 o'clock this morning. We
have barely space to say that he was tried by a jury of
the highest respectability, and was unquestionably guilty
of the crime for which he was condemned. The example
was terrible; but if it have the effect of ridding
us of the thieves who infest our city, and of the incen-
diaries and assassins by whom the lives of our citizens
are nightly threatened, we shall not regard the punishment
as too severe. There is reason to believe that
the unfortunate man was of the most hardened
