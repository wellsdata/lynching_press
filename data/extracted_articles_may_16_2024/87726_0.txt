LYNCH LAW IN INDIANA- On the night of the
7th instant a company of men, blacked and disguised
, paid James Madison Rush, a young gentleman
of Brownstown, Indiana, a visit- took him
from his bed and wife and lynched him soundly
with real hickory timber well seasoned. The
blood streamed from a hundred wounds before the
mob were satisfied to let their victim go. Rush
has not been able to walk since. The patry styled
themselves the Regulators, and charged Rush
with several larcenies. They informed him that
if he did not leave the place in ten days he
