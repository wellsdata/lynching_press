Mob Wanted Him.
Santiago De Cuba, Oct. 30.- Octavio
Mena, formerly a clerk in the office of
the captain of the port, was found
guilty of the murder, about a month
ago, of Colonel Pavoon of the Cuban
army, whom he killed in a drunken
row. The evidence
showed that the
homicide was entirely without provocation
. He will probably be sentenced
to imprisonment for 17 years and ordered
to pay to the family of his vicat
tim the sum of$ 2,000. While Mena
was being taken from the jail to
court room a mob tried to lynch him the.
