SHIPP'S TRIAL BEGUN.
Sheriff and Deputies Charged with
Contempt at Chattanooga, Tenn.
Special to The Washington Herald.
Chattanooga, Tenn., Feb. 12.- Hearing
in the contempt case of Sheriff J. F.
Shipp, several of his deputies and alleged
members of the mob that lynched
Johnson last March, was commenced
to- day before Special Commissioner J.
D. Maser.
Three newspaper men, a
former secretary of United States
Judge Clark, and a telegraph manager
were examined.
A sensation resulted when it was
found that two of the most important
witnesses for the government have disappeared
. These were A. J. Ware,
formerly justice of the peace, and Joe
Franklin, formerly a colored constable.
