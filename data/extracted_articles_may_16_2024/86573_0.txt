It is reported that Alcide Mouton, the
young negro lynched in St. Landry for
the murder of his wife, so- called, was a
disreputable character, having been
caught in various thefts and whipped
in consequence. He had been run out
of Avoyelles for an offense of this kind.
The woman whom he killed on account
of infidelity was not his wife, but
another man's and was forty years old,
while he was twenty,.
