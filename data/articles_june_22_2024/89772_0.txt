" Mills had learned that I accused him of

me about 2 o'clock yesterday afternoon and
asked me to go to Dr. Shields's office as he
wanted to prove something I had said. I
went with him and on arriving there he
said:' Baird, Dr. Shields says that you told
him I was in the mob that lynched you.' I
replied that I did say so and still think it.
Mills then drew his revolver and fired two
shots at me, neither of which took effect.
