John W. Collins, a young Florida
negro, shot and killed Michael Lynch,
a New York City policeman, on the
morning of July 1, 1911. Collins had
been drinking and fired a revolver
out of the window of his room in the
section of New York known as" Hell's
Kitchen." Policeman Lynch heard the
shots and went to investigate. When
knocked at the door Collins opened
it he and fired.
