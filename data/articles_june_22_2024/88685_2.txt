DAY'S DEVELOPMENTS
Developments during the day included:
Announcement by County Attorney A.
V. Shotwell that he would do everything
in bis power to fix the responsibility for
the rioting and to start prosecutions.
Issuance of a proclamation by Lieut.
Col. Jacob Wuest, commanding officer at
Fort Omaha, promising protection to all
and asking that firearms be given over
to the police or military authorities.
The stationing of federal troops near
the hospital where Mayor Smith lies
suffering from injuries received when a
mob attempted to lynch him.
Announcement by Mayor Smith's physician
that his condition was" very sat-
isfactory."
CITY REMAINS QUIET
Inauguration by local authorities of
fresh
precautions to guard against a
outbreak, including the transportation
of negro prisoners from the jail here to
the state penitentiary at Lincoln.
The city was quiet throughout the day,
soldiers patrolling the streets to prevent
the congregation of groups on street corners
.
Mayor Smith was the victim severing
years ago of an accident which required
a serious operation on the skull. He wears
a silver plate where a small portion of
the skull was removed at that time. During
the attempt to place his life in
jeopardy Sunday the mayor received a
severe blow on the head from the effects
of which danger of complications was
feared.
On account of his previous disability
the mayor had a long time refrained
from violent exercises for of all kinds.
