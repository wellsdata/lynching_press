ROCHESTER, April 8.- There was an exciting
finale to the lecture of Miss Ida B. Wells last
evening at the First Baptist Church, in which a
theological student from Texas and Miss Susan
B. Anthony participated. Miss Wells said that
negro lynching had increased in the South in a
marked degree; that between 1882 and 1892
1,000 colored people were lynched on slight
pretexts, and in many cases when they were
known to be innocent. She gave a number of
illustrations bearing upon her conclusion, when
the theological student, who said he hailed
from Texas, arose and said:
